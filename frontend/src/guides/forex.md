## _section_guide
Forex and CFD cost metrics show how much market friction eats into your returns. Spread is the bid-ask gap you pay on every entry and exit — it is the primary cost for most retail forex trading. Swap (overnight financing) accumulates on positions held past the daily rollover. For high-frequency strategies, spread cost dominates; for swing strategies, swap cost matters more. Compare total costs with gross PnL to see what percentage of your edge goes to friction.

## total_pips
Total pips gained or lost across all closed trades. A pip is the standard unit of price movement (0.0001 for most FX pairs, 0.01 for JPY pairs). Positive means net profitable in pip terms.

## avg_pips_per_trade
Average pips per trade. Indicates how much price movement the strategy captures per position. Strategies averaging under 5 pips per trade are highly sensitive to spread costs and execution quality.

## total_spread_cost
Total cost paid in spread (bid-ask gap) across all trades. Spread is charged on both entry and exit. This is usually the largest cost component for forex/CFD trading.

## total_swap_cost
Total overnight swap (financing) charges accumulated on positions held past the daily rollover time. Can be positive (earning swap) or negative (paying swap) depending on the interest rate differential and position direction.
